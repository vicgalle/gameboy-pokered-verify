# Bug: 1/256 Miss Chance on "100% Accuracy" Moves

## Gameplay Description

In Pokemon Red/Blue, moves that are supposed to have 100% accuracy (like Swift or
most Normal-type moves at full accuracy) can still miss with a probability of
approximately 1 in 256. This means that even guaranteed-hit moves occasionally fail.

The same pattern also affects critical hit calculations — there is a 1/256 chance
that the maximum possible critical hit rate still fails to produce a critical hit.

## Root Cause

The game generates a random byte (0-255) and compares it against a threshold to
determine if a move hits or if a critical hit occurs. The comparison uses a strict
inequality where a non-strict one is needed. This means when the random value
exactly equals the threshold, the outcome is incorrectly treated as a failure
instead of a success.

## Where to Look

- The accuracy check is in `engine/battle/core.asm`
- Search for labels related to move hit testing (e.g., `MoveHitTest` or similar)
- The critical hit check is also in `core.asm` near `CriticalHitTest`
- Both use a `call BattleRandom` followed by a `cp` instruction and a conditional
  jump/return
- The `cp` instruction sets the carry flag when A < operand; the conditional
  branch then acts on carry/no-carry
- Also look at the `percent` macro in `macros/` for how thresholds are defined

## Severity

Low per-occurrence (only 1/256 chance) but statistically significant over thousands
of battles. Affects all moves and all critical hit checks in the game.
